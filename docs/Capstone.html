Capstone Option

Regardless of whether you add a capstone component to this course, you will complete a final paper and presentation which are based on a proposed project. If you add the capstone component to this course, you will additionally be expected to implement the design of your proposed final project using experiment software (with my supervision), collect data for this project (even informally from a few friends), complete basic data analysis of the project, and write up the actual results (rather than just anticipated results).

If you choose to take this course with a capstone designation, you must complete the additional paperwork and submit it to the Psychology department. For the description of the project you can write the following: design experiment on psychology of language, collect and analyze data, interpret results, and write about findings.

Because the capstone option is highly integrated with the final project, I have integrated both sets of milestones and expectations here. If you do not take this course with a capstone option, you will only be expected to complete the milestones and expectations outlined in the Final paper and presentation documentation.

There are eight milestones for the capstone project:

  1. By 10/19 at 2:10PM, submit a short proposal (1/2-1 page) of a specific research question you will explore in the final paper and presentation. The proposal should detail some questions that you are interested in investigating. It should also include at least two empirical papers (outside of course materials) that you plan to read. I highly suggest you spend some time before this deadline beginning to think about this project so I can give you specific feedback and get you on the right track from the beginning. You can submit one or two ideas for feedback.

  2. By 10/31 at 2:10PM, sign up for free account on gorilla.sc, complete introduction and tutorial workshops, and submit a detailed design and analysis plan. The design and analysis plan should connect your proposed research experiment to an explicit experimental design with sufficient detail about dependent/independent variables and experimental hypothesis/hypotheses. You should also give detail about the specific summary statistics (e.g., means, standard deviations) that you will report about your independent variable(s). Finally, you should explain the statistical test you propose to use to test your experimental hypothesis.

  3. By 11/16 at 2:10PM, share your experiment with me on Gorilla by adding me as a collaborator. On your project, click Settings at the top right, then Collaborate. Search for Casey Roark and then add to project. You should use Casey Roark not Casey Roark (CMU). Make sure that both Make Changes and View Data have the green checkmark selected.

  4. By 11/15 at 9PM, complete a detailed outline or rough draft (~3-6 pages) of the final paper that will be peer-reviewed by a classmate and reviewed by me. This outline/draft should include the research question and any sub-questions, a detailed overview of the experimental method that you would use to answer the research question, anticipated/hypothesized results. You should cite at least five empirical sources (outside of course materials).

  5. By 11/16 at 9PM (especially during the class period on 11/16), peer-edit the assigned paper. Read the draft using track changes in Microsoft Word to make any suggestions or comments. Write down any question you have – were any parts confusing? Write down parts that you liked. It is possible that these edits will not be anonymized, so keep that in mind. Submit the edited draft to Canvas.

  6. By 11/30 (Th 11/30, T 12/5, Th 12/7), you should prepare a 5-7 minute presentation about your proposed project. You should include an introduction that lays out the purpose/question of the planned experiment, the method of the planned experiment, and the anticipated/hypothesized results. You should be prepared to answer questions about your presentation.

  7. By 12/19 at 12:30PM, you must collect data from at least five individuals through Gorilla. After you design your experiment (with my supervision and approval), you can collect pilot data from five participants by clicking on your experiment, clicking on Preview experiment and having them run through all experiment parts. At the very end – be sure to manually save your data as a csv or xlsx file or else your data will be lost! Share these data files in myCourses for the Capstone project.

  8. By 12/19 at 12:30PM, you should submit a final draft of the paper, incorporating feedback from the outline/draft. The paper should be at least 2500 words long (~ 10 double spaced pages in 12 pt font, not including references or title page). The paper should be in APA format.

The paper should have the following parts:

  • Title (on its own page)
  • Abstract (on same pages as other sections below)
  • Background and significance
  • Method
    • Hypothesis/hypotheses
    • Experimental design
  • Results
    • Description of analyses run
    • Summary statistics
    • Detailed experimental results
    • Figures
  • Discussion
    • Interpretation of results in relation to experimental hypothesis
    • Limitations
  • References
